Blood Lead Levels in NYC

Context

For my Data Journalism Project, I looked at the lead levels in New York City children's blood over a period from 2005 to 2016. The New York City government has put a lot of effort into lead remediation, and this project shows the effects this has had on the health of children in NYC.

Working Process

1)Topic

Brfore starting this project, I had learned and was familiar with the issue of lead remediation in New York City from a Biology Presentation I did on that topic. I then found a data set that focused on that issue, and started from there.

2) Designing a Prototype

After picking my topic of lead levels in blood, I made a cursory prototype of what my project would end up looking like, and got feedback on it from my peers.

3) Data Cleansing

After receiving peer feedback I narrowed the scope of my project to make it more easily understandable to a first-time viewer.
I then inmported the data, took the parts I was going to use, then made it into a .json file so it can be read by the program.

4) Coding

With my peer's feedback on my sketches in mind, I began to work on two different representations of my data.
The first is a map of NYC that is shaded in with values representing the most recent year in the dataset, 2016.
The second is a line chart that shows the progress over time by borough of lead remediation as reflected in blood testing of children living in NYC

Prototype

NYC View

Design Reasoning: I figured that a good way to represent lead exposure across NYC was to use a shaded map to express the latest data.
I found this to be the most straightforward way as well one that told an easily understandable story.
I thought that a line graph showing values over time was the best way to show the city's progress on lead remediation since 2005.
